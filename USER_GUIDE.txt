================================================================================
  IP-A1 VOLUME CONTROLLER — USER GUIDE
================================================================================
For teachers, staff, and administrators who use the app day-to-day.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT IS THIS APP?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
IP-A1 Volume Controller is a simple web tool that lets you control the volume
of your classroom ceiling speakers from your phone, tablet, or computer —
without touching a physical amplifier or installing any app.

It runs on a small program on a Windows PC in your school. Once it's running,
anyone on the same Wi-Fi network can open it in their browser and control the
speakers in their room.

Think of it like a remote control for your speakers, that lives in a web browser.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHAT YOU NEED BEFORE STARTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On the Windows PC that runs the app:
  1. Node.js installed (free download from https://nodejs.org — get the LTS version)
     - After installing, restart the PC once
     - To check it worked: open Command Prompt and type:  node --version
       You should see something like v20.0.0

  2. The IP-A1 Volume Controller folder (provided by your IT team or developer)
     - This is the folder containing start.bat and the rest of the program files

Your school network:
  - The speakers must be connected to the same Wi-Fi or network as the PC
  - Each speaker must have its own fixed IP address (set up by your IT team)
  - You will need the IP address and login password for each speaker

That's it. No special software is needed on phones or tablets — just a browser.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STARTING THE APP (WINDOWS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open the IP-A1 Volume Controller folder on the PC
2. Double-click  start.bat
3. A black window (Command Prompt) will appear — this is normal, leave it open
4. The first time you run it, it may take about 30 seconds to build the app
   (you will see text scrolling in the black window)
5. After a few seconds, your web browser will automatically open to a page
   showing a QR code — this is the Connection Page (explained below)

IMPORTANT: Keep the black Command Prompt window open. If you close it,
the app stops working. You can minimize it, just don't close it.

To stop the app: close the black Command Prompt window, or press Ctrl+C inside it.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE CONNECTION PAGE (QR CODE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When the app starts, your browser opens to the Connection Page.
This page shows:

  - A large QR CODE
  - The NETWORK ADDRESS (e.g. http://192.168.1.50:5000)
  - Instructions for connecting other devices

WHAT IS THE QR CODE?
  The QR code contains the web address of the app on your school's network.
  When a phone or tablet scans it, the browser on that device opens the app
  automatically — no typing required.

HOW TO CONNECT A PHONE OR TABLET:
  Method 1 — QR Code (easiest):
    1. Open the Camera app on the phone/tablet (or use a QR scanner app)
    2. Point it at the QR code on the screen
    3. Tap the notification/link that appears
    4. The app opens in the phone's browser

  Method 2 — Type the address:
    1. Open any web browser on the phone/tablet
    2. Type the address shown on the Connection Page (e.g. http://192.168.1.50:5000)
    3. The app opens

  IMPORTANT: The phone/tablet must be connected to the SAME Wi-Fi network as
  the PC. It will NOT work on mobile data (4G/5G).

The PC's browser can also navigate to the main app at any time:
  Click "Go to App" on the Connection Page, or type  http://localhost:5000


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SAVING A SHORTCUT ON YOUR PHONE OR TABLET (APP-LIKE EXPERIENCE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
You can save the IP-A1 Controller to your phone's home screen so it opens
just like a real app — with its own icon, no browser address bar.

ON iPHONE / iPAD (Safari):
  1. Open the app in Safari on your iPhone/iPad
  2. Tap the Share button (the box with an arrow pointing up)
  3. Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen"
  4. Give it a name (e.g. "Room Control") and tap "Add"
  5. The app icon now appears on your home screen — tap it to open like an app!

ON ANDROID PHONE / TABLET (Chrome):
  1. Open the app in Chrome on your Android device
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner
  3. Tap "Add to Home screen"
  4. Tap "Add" when prompted
  5. The icon appears on your home screen

Once saved, opening the app from the home screen removes the browser address
bar and gives a full-screen, app-like experience.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE MAIN SCREEN — YOUR ROOMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When you open the app, you see the Rooms screen.
Each room is shown as a card (tile) with the room name.

  - A room with ONE speaker shows the speaker's IP address below the name
  - A room with MULTIPLE SPEAKERS shows a number badge on the speaker icon
    (e.g. a small "2" or "3") and lists the speaker names

Tap any room card to open the volume controls for that room.

If you have more than 5 rooms, a SEARCH BAR appears at the top.
You can search by room name, speaker label, or IP address.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CONTROLLING VOLUME IN A ROOM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When you tap a room, you see the Control Panel for that room.

AT THE TOP:
  - Back arrow (←) to go back to the room list
  - Room name
  - Connection status:
      • Green dot = speaker connected and responding
      • Red dot = speaker unreachable (check it's powered on and on the network)

IN THE MIDDLE:
  A large circular dial shows the current volume as a percentage (0% to 100%).
  Below it:

  SLIDER: Drag left/right to set volume precisely
  MINUS (−) BUTTON: Decrease volume by one step
  PLUS (+) BUTTON: Increase volume by one step

  MUTE / UNMUTE BUTTON:
    - Tap to mute the speaker (button turns red, says "Unmute Speaker")
    - Tap again to restore volume (button returns to normal)
    - Muting does NOT change the volume setting — it just silences the speaker.
      The volume setting is remembered when you unmute.

AT THE BOTTOM — PRESETS:
  Three quick-access buttons for common volume levels:

    LOW     = 25% volume  — quiet background audio
    NORMAL  = 50% volume  — clear speech, normal classroom use
    LOUD    = 78% volume  — louder presentations or noisy environments

  Tap a preset to instantly jump to that volume.
  The active preset is highlighted in orange.

CLASSROOM EXAMPLE:
  You are a teacher in Room 101. You open the app on your phone, tap "Room 101",
  and see the speaker is connected (green dot). Before class, you tap NORMAL to
  set a comfortable volume. During a video, you slide it up higher. At the end
  of class, you tap MUTE to silence it while students leave.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ROOMS WITH MULTIPLE SPEAKERS (GROUPED SPEAKERS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If a room has more than one ceiling speaker (e.g. a large gym or auditorium
with both a front speaker and a rear speaker), they can all be controlled
from one place.

SYNCED MODE (default — link icon in header):
  All speakers in the room are controlled together.
  When you move the slider or tap a preset, ALL speakers change at the same time.
  "Mute All" silences every speaker in the room instantly.

  Below the controls, you will see a status list showing each speaker's name,
  IP address, and a green/red dot indicating if it is connected.

  CLASSROOM EXAMPLE (GYM):
    Room "Main Gym" has 4 speakers (Front Left, Front Right, Rear Left, Rear Right).
    You tap "Mute All" before an assembly starts. When ready, tap "Unmute All"
    and all four speakers come back on at the same level. You drag the slider to
    set the volume for the whole room.

INDIVIDUAL MODE (unlink icon in header):
  Tap the "Synced" button in the top-right of the control panel to switch to
  "Individual" mode.
  Each speaker now has its own separate controls:
    - Its own volume slider and +/− buttons
    - Its own mute button
    - Its own Low/Normal/Loud presets
  This lets you make the front speakers louder than the rear, for example.

  CLASSROOM EXAMPLE (THEATRE):
    The school theatre has a front speaker over the stage and a rear speaker
    at the back for students sitting far away. In Individual mode, you can set
    the front speaker to Normal and the rear speaker to Loud, so everyone
    hears equally well.

To switch back to Synced mode, tap the "Individual" button in the header.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ADMIN MODE — MANAGING ROOMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Adding, editing, or deleting rooms is protected by an admin password.
This prevents students or other users from accidentally changing the setup.

TO UNLOCK ADMIN MODE:
  1. Tap the lock icon (🔒) in the top-right corner of the Rooms screen
  2. Enter the admin password:  IPA1
  3. Tap "Unlock"
  The lock icon turns orange (🔓) and the "Add Room" button appears.

TO LOCK ADMIN MODE AGAIN:
  Tap the orange lock icon — it locks instantly with no password needed.

ADMIN MODE FEATURES:
  - Add Room: create a new room with one or more speakers
  - Edit Room (pencil icon on tile): change room name or speaker details
  - Delete Room (trash icon on tile): tap once to arm, tap "Delete?" to confirm

ADDING A NEW ROOM:
  1. Unlock admin mode
  2. Tap "Add Room" (or the + tile at the end of the grid)
  3. Enter a Room Name (e.g. "Science Lab")
  4. Fill in the Speaker details:
       - Label: a name for this speaker (e.g. "Main Speaker" or "Front")
       - IP Address: the speaker's network address (e.g. 192.168.1.105)
         (ask your IT team if you don't know this)
       - Username: the speaker's login username (usually "admin")
       - Password: the speaker's login password (ask your IT team)
  5. To add a second speaker to the same room, tap "+ Add Speaker"
     and fill in its details
  6. Tap "Add Room" to save

Room settings are saved automatically and shared across all devices.
If you add a room on the PC, anyone connected on their phone will see it too
after refreshing.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HOW SPEAKER CREDENTIALS ARE STORED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Speaker IP addresses, usernames, and passwords are stored in a file called
rooms.json on the PC where the app runs.

These credentials are NOT sent to or stored on any other device or the internet.
They are only used by the PC to communicate with the speakers on your LAN.
Mobile devices and tablets never directly see the speaker passwords.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TROUBLESHOOTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM: Red dot next to room name / "Speaker unreachable" message
  SOLUTION:
    1. Check that the speaker is powered on (light on the speaker unit)
    2. Check that the speaker's network cable is connected (if wired)
    3. Make sure the PC and speaker are on the same network
    4. Ask IT to verify the IP address of the speaker hasn't changed
    5. Try refreshing the page

PROBLEM: Cannot open the app on my phone
  SOLUTION:
    1. Make sure your phone is on the SAME Wi-Fi as the PC (not mobile data)
    2. Try typing the address manually (e.g. http://192.168.1.50:5000)
    3. Make sure the black Command Prompt window is still open on the PC
    4. Try scanning the QR code again from the Connection Page

PROBLEM: Volume change doesn't seem to work
  SOLUTION:
    1. Check the speaker connection (red/green dot in the app)
    2. Try pressing the − or + button instead of the slider
    3. Check if the speaker is muted (red mute button) — unmute first

PROBLEM: My rooms disappeared
  SOLUTION:
    1. If the app shows "Local only — tap to retry", tap it to reconnect
    2. Make sure the black Command Prompt window is still open on the PC
    3. Try refreshing the page — room list will reload from the server

PROBLEM: The app opens to a blank page
  SOLUTION:
    1. Close the Command Prompt window
    2. Delete the "dist" folder inside the app folder
    3. Double-click start.bat again to rebuild

PROBLEM: I forgot the admin password
  SOLUTION: The password is  IPA1  (capital letters, no spaces)
  If it was changed, ask your IT administrator or developer.
